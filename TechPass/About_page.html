Department Overview

The Department of Computing and Information Science (formerly Department of Computer Science) is a degree-granting department under the College of Arts and Sciences offering Bachelor of Science in Computer Science, Bachelor of Science in Information Technology and Master in Information Technology. The department is composed of faculty members who handle both major and non-major subjects as well as CS and IT major subjects.

Goals

The program aims to develop a well-rounded individual while focusing on the acquisition of knowledge and skills that will enable them to effectively use technical skills to develop concrete solutions to problems specifically in the field of computing sciences, information technology and systems analysis and development.

Objectives

  • To produce students with the theoretical and practical skills that will enable them to be globally competitive as computer science professionals;
  • To prepare students for careers in computing sciences, information technology, and computer education;
  • To generate students with the professional expertise to enable them to assume relevant positions in business, government, and the academe;
  • To enable students to develop researches and engage them in lifelong learning;
  • To develop in the students social, political, and environmental values so as to become better members of the society.
